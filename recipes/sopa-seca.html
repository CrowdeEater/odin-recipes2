Sopa Seca de Arroz, or Mexican Rice

This is an excelled authentic Mexican Rice recipe (not to be confused with Spanish rice) that I make as a side dish with all of my Mexican dishes. The key is cooking the rice properly and using good quality chicken broth

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 white onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  1. Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat and add rice.
  2. Cook, stirring constantly, until puffed and golden.
  3. As rice is beginning to turn golden, stir in the salt and cumin.
  4. Stir in onions and continue cooking 2 minutes, or until they just begin to soften.
  5. Add the tomato paste and continue cooking 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  6. Stir in the chicken broth and bring to a boil
  7. Reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked.
  8. Note: The rice may still be wet even though it is fully cooked. If it is wet, remove from heat and replace the lid. Let rice sit for 10 minutes.
  9. Fluff the rice with a fork and serve.
